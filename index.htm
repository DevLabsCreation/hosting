Hosting a Website on GitHub

Step 1: Create a GitHub Account

1. Go to GitHub.com and click on "Sign up" in the top right corner.

2. Fill out the registration form with your details and click on "Create account."

3. Verify your email address by clicking on the link sent to you by GitHub.

Step 2: Create a New Repository

1. Log in to your GitHub account and click on the "+" icon in the top right corner.

2. Select "New repository" and fill out the repository name and description.

3. Choose "Public" or "Private" depending on your needs and click on "Create repository."

Step 3: Upload Your Website Files

1. Go to your repository and click on "Add file" > "Upload files."

2. Drag and drop your website files (HTML, CSS, JS, images, etc.) into the upload area.

3. Commit the changes with a meaningful message.

Step 4: Configure GitHub Pages

1. Go to your repository settings and scroll down to "GitHub Pages."

2. Select the branch you want to use for GitHub Pages (usually "main" or "master").

3. Save the changes and wait for GitHub to build your website.

Step 5: Visit Your Website

1. Once GitHub Pages is configured, you can visit your website at https://your-username.github.io/your-repo-name.

2. Replace "your-username" and "your-repo-name" with your actual GitHub username and repository name.
